Student Life

Extracurricular Activities

Extracurricular activities at the University of Pittsburgh (Pitt) offer students an opportunity to explore their passions, develop leadership skills, and connect with peers who share similar interests. With over 600 student organizations on campus, there’s something for everyone—from academic clubs and pre-professional societies to arts, culture, and service-oriented groups. Students interested in expanding their academic horizons can join groups like the American Marketing Association, the Pitt Robotics and Automation Society, or the Student Government Board, where they can gain practical experience and leadership opportunities outside of the classroom.

Pitt also encourages students to engage in recreational and athletic activities. Club and intramural sports are popular, offering students a chance to stay active while competing in a more casual, social setting. Options range from soccer and basketball to unique sports like ultimate frisbee and fencing. The university’s fitness and recreation centers provide facilities for exercise and wellness, while outdoor adventures like hiking and kayaking are accessible through Pitt’s Outdoors Club. These activities promote a healthy lifestyle and help students balance the demands of academic life with physical well-being.

Community service and civic engagement play a key role in Pitt’s extracurricular landscape as well. Students are encouraged to participate in volunteer activities through organizations like PittServes, which connects students with service opportunities both on and off-campus. Events like Pitt Make a Difference Day allow students to give back to the Pittsburgh community while fostering a sense of social responsibility. Through these extracurricular activities, Pitt students can explore their interests, make lasting friendships, and contribute to their community, enriching their overall college experience.
